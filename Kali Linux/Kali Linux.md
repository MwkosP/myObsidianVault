It came from the team that created [BackTrack Linux](https://el.wikipedia.org/wiki/BackTrack_Linux "BackTrack Linux") . The team chose to name it after the Indian goddess [Kali](https://el.wikipedia.org/wiki/%CE%9A%CE%AC%CE%BB%CE%B9 "Kali") . [[ 3 ]](https://el.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kali_Linux#cite_note-3)

The distribution was designed from the ground up. This gave them the freedom to abandon [Ubuntu](https://el.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubuntu "Ubuntu") and choose Debian as their base distribution, which they now rely on (it is currently based on [Debian Wheezy](https://el.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debian_Wheezy?action=edit&redlink=1 "Debian Wheezy (not yet written)") ).

Apart from a few paths, which changed due to the [Filesystem Hierarchy Standard](https://el.wikipedia.org/wiki/Filesystem_Hierarchy_Standard_\(FHS\)?action=edit&redlink=1 "Filesystem Hierarchy Standard (FHS) (not yet written)") specifications , BackTrack users will not have to deal with a very different environment. [[ 4 ]](https://el.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kali_Linux#cite_note-4)

It contains many tools for network penetration testing and is available for free. All packages, as well as the distribution itself, are [GPG](https://el.wikipedia.org/wiki/GPG?action=edit&redlink=1 "GPG (not yet written)") signed .
