



# Bittensor — Token (TAO)

---

## Overview

TAO is the native token of Bittensor. It is simultaneously:

- **Currency** — used to pay registration fees, stake, and transact
- **Stake** — determines influence over rankings and emissions
- **Reward** — distributed to participants who produce useful intelligence
- **Governance signal** — stake = voting power over the network's direction

TAO is not just a financial instrument. It is the **mechanism by which the network decides what intelligence is valuable**. More TAO = more say in what gets rewarded. This makes TAO's distribution inseparable from the network's long-term quality.

---

## 1. Supply — The Hard Cap

TAO has a **maximum supply of 21 million**, deliberately mirroring Bitcoin:

$$\text{Max Supply} = 21,000,000 \text{ TAO}$$

This is hardcoded into the protocol. No entity — including the Opentensor Foundation — can mint TAO beyond this cap. Scarcity is enforced at the protocol level.

### Why 21 million?

The Bitcoin supply model was chosen deliberately to signal:

- Predictable, auditable monetary policy
- No inflation beyond the emission schedule
- Resistance to political or organizational pressure to expand supply

---

## 2. Emission Schedule

### Block-by-block emission

TAO is minted every block at a rate of `τ` TAO per block. At genesis this was set to:

$$\tau_0 = 1 \text{ TAO per block}$$

Bittensor produces approximately **1 block every 12 seconds**, meaning:

$$\text{Daily emission} \approx \frac{86400}{12} \times 1 = 7200 \text{ TAO/day at genesis}$$

### Halving schedule

Like Bitcoin, TAO undergoes **halvings** — the emission rate cuts in half at regular intervals:

$$\tau \rightarrow \frac{\tau}{2} \quad \text{every } 10.5 \text{ million blocks}$$

At 12 seconds per block, 10.5 million blocks ≈ **~4 years**

|Halving|Emission (TAO/block)|Approximate Year|
|---|---|---|
|Genesis|1.0|2023|
|1st Halving|0.5|~2027|
|2nd Halving|0.25|~2031|
|3rd Halving|0.125|~2035|
|...|...|...|

Total supply approaches but never exceeds 21M as emissions geometrically decay.

---

## 3. How TAO Enters Circulation

TAO is not pre-mined or allocated to a foundation treasury at launch in large quantities. It enters circulation **only through work**:

```
Block produced
      ↓
τ TAO minted
      ↓
Root Network (SN0) allocates to subnets
      ↓
Within each subnet — Yuma Consensus runs
      ↓
TAO split: 18% Owner / 41% Validators / 41% Miners
      ↓
Participants accumulate stake
```

This means every TAO in existence was earned by a participant doing something the network judged as valuable. There is no VC allocation, no founder pre-mine in the standard sense, no airdrop pool.

---

## 4. Stake — TAO as Power

Holding TAO is not passive. Stake is **active capital** in Bittensor:

### What stake does

|Function|Effect|
|---|---|
|Validator weight|Your `sᵢ` in `R = WᵀS` — more stake = more influence over miner rankings|
|Emission weight|Your share of validator emissions scales with `sᵢ · Tᵢ`|
|Root Network voting|Top validators by stake rank subnets in SN0|
|Registration|Required minimum to join subnets as validator|

### Staking mechanics

- TAO can be **staked to a validator** (delegated) or self-staked
- Delegators earn a share of validator emissions proportional to their contribution
- Validators can set a **take rate** — the % of delegator earnings they keep as fee
- Delegating to a well-performing validator is a way to earn yield on TAO without running infrastructure

### Staking is not locking

Unlike many PoS chains, TAO stake can be **unstaked relatively quickly**. This keeps capital mobile and allows the market to reallocate stake toward better validators rapidly.

---

## 5. TAO Burn Mechanics

TAO is burned (permanently removed from supply) in several scenarios:

|Event|Amount Burned|
|---|---|
|Subnet creation|Variable — scales with network demand|
|Neuron registration (miner/validator)|Small fixed amount per registration|
|Recycled low-rank emissions|Unclaimed or dust emissions may be recycled/burned|

Burning is **deflationary** — it reduces circulating supply. As the network grows and more subnets are created and more participants register, burn rate increases. This partially offsets new emission.

The net effect: early in the network's life, emission > burn (inflationary). As the network matures and registration activity grows, burn increases and net inflation decreases.

---

## 6. TAO as a Proxy for Intelligence Value

This is the key philosophical point:

> The market price of TAO is, in theory, a market valuation of the aggregate intelligence the network produces.

If Bittensor's subnets are producing intelligence that people find genuinely useful:

- Demand for TAO increases (needed to access subnet outputs, register, stake)
- Supply is fixed at 21M
- Price rises

If the network produces low-quality or easily gamed intelligence:

- Demand falls
- Validators and miners exit
- Price falls

TAO price is therefore a **continuous external signal** on whether the Darwinian incentive system is actually producing real intelligence — or just being farmed.

## 7. Delegation and Yield

For TAO holders who don't want to run infrastructure:

### Delegating to validators

- Choose a validator to delegate stake to
- Validator uses your stake in `R = WᵀS`, increasing their ranking power
- You earn a share of their validator emissions minus their take rate
- Risk: if the validator ranks dishonestly (low trust `T`), your yield drops

### Yield calculation (approximate)

$$\text{Delegator yield} \approx \frac{s_{\text{delegated}}}{s_{\text{validator total}}} \times E_{\text{validator}} \times (1 - \text{take rate})$$

- Higher stake validators earn more in absolute terms but dilute per-TAO yield
- Smaller validators with high trust can offer better yield per TAO delegated
- This creates a **validator market** — delegators shop for the best risk-adjusted yield

---

## 8. TAO vs Subnet Tokens (dTAO)

In Bittensor's evolving architecture, individual subnets can issue their own tokens — called **dTAO** (dynamic TAO) or subnet-specific tokens:

- Each subnet has its own token that represents stake within that subnet
- TAO can be converted into subnet tokens and back
- This creates a **two-layer token economy**:
    - TAO = global layer, global influence
    - Subnet tokens = local layer, influence within one subnet

### Why this matters

- It allows subnet-level price discovery — the market can value each subnet independently
- Miners and validators can be paid in subnet tokens, convertible to TAO
- Creates capital flows between subnets based on relative performance
- Still in development / progressive rollout as of 2024
